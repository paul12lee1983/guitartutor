About Me...

I am a professional musician and I’ve been a guitar teacher in and around the Swindon area for over 20 years but my love of the guitar has been a life long passion. I can’t remember a time when I couldn’t get a tune out of the instrument - it really has been my focus from a very early age.

After many years sat in my bedroom as a small boy trying to ﬁgure out how to play chords and the riffs of the time, my parents suggested I join the Guitar Club at my Junior School and that was the beginning of years of tuition. Without the help of local guitar teachers I’m sure I would not know what I know today - I’m grateful for the help and guidance I received from all of them! In Secondary School I was one of just a handful of pupils who took his electric guitar in to school each day to make music with friends and eventually form a band.

Today, it seems that every other child is carrying an instrument of some sort and this is always great to see. As learning an instrument becomes evermore popular today so does the demand for music teachers with genuine passion for helping the next wave of musicians make their mark on the music scene. I’ve enjoyed teaching well over a thousand guitarists over the years, seeing many of them progress from playing their first chord to going on stage and inspiring others to take up the challenge of learning how to play guitar. Teaching those with a passion for the guitar is a great job - actually it rarely feels like a job at all!

My studio is located in North Swindon with good access and parking. All lesson are one to one. With recent events due to the pandemic, the studio is COVID-19 safe with cleaning after each session and a protective screen between myself and the student.

Andy is a very capable and friendly guitar teacher who really knows his stuff when it comes to playing and teaching. He taught many of the students at Greendown School when I was Head of Music and helped to set up a ‘Rock School’ which was hugely popular.
Andy and I have worked together on many occasions in both theatres, school productions and also studio sessions. I would always recommend Andy to anyone wanting to learn the electric guitar and he’s always my first choice if I need a great guitar player in the orchestra pit! Andre Pysanczyn - Music Teacher / Composer

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you teach beginners?

Yes, I teach all abilities and ages. Everyone will be welcomed. Lessons will be tailored for your requirements and music style.

What guitar styles do you teach?

Anything and everything. From acoustic and rock.

Do I need a guitar?

Yes but I do have a selection of guitars you can use during the lesson if you require one, but having your own will help for home practice and familiarity of the instrument. I also have guitar effects and a variety of guitar accessories available in the studio to help you achieve distinctive sounds.
If you are looking to buy your first guitar or purchase a new one, I can also assist you and help you decide which guitar would be best suited for you.

Can I make a recording of my progress?

Yes, I can make a high quality recording of your playing. I can produce professional quality music for any genre and composition. I can help you progress through recordings and together we can compose your own music if you wish to do so.

Do I pay for cancelled lessons?

I require at least 24 hours notice to cancel a session with no charge. Less than 24 hours will be charged at the normal rate. Option for re-scheduling can be made subject to availability.

Are you CRB checked?

Yes - I teach in a number of local schools which mandates this requirement.

Can I study grading?

That is totally your decision. I teach all types of students with different ambitions. If you decide taking grading exams is for you, then I will help you achieve your goal.
